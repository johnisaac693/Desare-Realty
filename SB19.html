SB19's Japan concert is finally happening in April

Published January 26, 2024

SB19 is finally pushing through with their show in Japan!

The P-Pop Kings announced the rescheduled date for their upcoming concert. It will be happening on April 29 still at the Japan Pavilion, Tokorozawa Sakura Town in Saitama, Japan.

The PAGTATAG! World Tour: Japan concert was originally scheduled for December 9, but was postponed. Tickets for that show date remain valid.

SB19 apologized for the long wait and told fans through 1Z Entertainment, “We can’t wait to provide you with an unforgettable experience.”

“Thank you for your understanding, and we look forward to celebrating this long-awaited moment together on April 29,” the group added.

In 2023, SB19 left their agency ShowBT Entertainment. They launched their own company 1Z Entertainment in October, with the band’s leader Pablo as the CEO.

Due to the conflict, SB19 canceled their concerts in Singapore, Bangkok, and Dubai.

Member Stell said the band and their former agency have communicated properly and resolved their conflict. Their first public appearance after the issue was at the 2023 Asia Artist Awards.

SB19 debuted in 2018. In their five years as a group, they released top hits like “GENTO,” went on world tours, appeared on US morning shows, and made history as the first Filipino act to be nominated for a Billboard Music Award, among many other achievements.
